英语3

Posted on 2018-06-11 | In word_power

cosmopolitan adj 世界性的，见多识广的，超越民W族偏见的，广布的，四海为家的
eg:International football punishes inward-looking（adj 只关注自身的，封闭的） countries and rewards those with more cosmopolitan attitudes.
翻译：国际足联惩罚只关注自身的国家 ，奖励那些超越民族偏见的行为。
qualified adj 合格的，合适的，有资格的 a qualified teacher 一个合格的教师
有保留的，附带条件的，限制性的
I have just got a qualified approval.我只得到保留的认可

eg:Only four countries rated “not free” by Freedom House,a charity,have qualified for this year’s World Cup,and none is likely to get far.
翻译：只有四个国家被注明不自由被自由屋已经限制其参加今年的世界杯，没有一个可能获得长远来看。

eg:The flags of Argentina and Brazil,that is-Bangladesh’s
national team is ranked 197th out of 207 in the world and has never qualified for the World Cup.
翻译：阿根廷和巴西的国旗，那是孟加拉的国旗。国家队在世界207个国家中排名第一百九十七，还没有进入世界杯的资格。
charity 慈善，仁爱，慈爱，宽容，救济金，
eg:first cases of Ebola were confirmed in Bikoro,a urgent request came into the headquarters（总部） of Medecins Sans Fronties,an international charity.
翻译：第一例埃博拉感染者被发现在来自国际慈善机构的紧急求助
sublime 壮丽的，宏伟的，极端的，惊人的
eg：The East trained massively muscled shot-piitters;the
West,sublime shot-makers.
flair 天赋
eg:But dictatoorships are rubbish at football,which requires more creativity and flair.
翻译：但是独裁是无意义的东西对于足球来说更需要的是创造力和天赋。
pageant 盛大的庆典，盛装的游行 an empty pageant空洞的虚饰
eg:Autocratic(专制的，独裁的) regimes such as China and Russia can ruthlessly drill track-and-field athletes-indeed,the Olympic games sometimes resemble an authoritarian pageant.
翻译：残酷的训练运动员，奥林匹克运动会有时像一场威权盛会。
authoritarian 权力主义的，独裁主义的，专制的 an authoritarian
government 专制政府 an authoritarian leader独裁主义的领导
resemble 同…类似的，与…类似的，与…外表相同
resemble each other in appearance or nature
ruthlessly 没有同情心的，冷酷的，残忍的
eg:One of ISraeli interrogators recalls him as “extremely hardline and at the same time ruthlessly pragmatic”
eg:Serious candidates who tried to stand against him were barred from doing so-and then ruthlessly punished.
autocrat 独裁者，专制君主，专横霸道的人
Autocratic regimes such as China and Russia can ruthlessly
drill track-and-field athletes-indeed,the Olympic games sometimes resemble an authoritarian.
opposed 独立的，相反的，反对的，对抗的，与…对照，相比之下
Yet the competition itself,as opposed to the murky process
of deciding where it is played,showcases progress.
